But when they factor in: - **$30,000 for housing & utilities** - **$15,000 for healthcare** (including a private Medicare supplement) - **$10,000 for travel & lifestyle** - **$5,000 for emergencies** …they realize they need **$75,000+** to account for **inflation and unexpected costs**. ### **4. Expected Social Security Benefits** - The calculator estimates your **monthly Social Security check** based on your **work history and earnings**. - *Example:* If you’ve contributed **$500,000** over 40 years, your **monthly benefit** might be **$2,500** (but this varies by age and contribution level). ### **5. Pension or Annuity Income (If Applicable)** - Some workers have **defined benefit pensions** that provide **lifetime income**. - *Example:* A teacher with a **$3,000/month pension** at age 65 can reduce their required savings. ### **6. Current Savings & Investment Portfolio** - The calculator assesses: - **401(k), IRA, Roth IRA balances** - **Stocks, bonds, real estate, or other assets** - **Expected annual returns** (historically, **7% is a safe long-term average** for diversified portfolios) *Real-World Example:* A **self-employed freelancer** has: - **$200,000 in a 401(k)** - **$50,000 in a Roth IRA** - **$150,000 in a rental property** The calculator projects **$300,000 in liquid savings** but warns that **real estate liquidity is limited**, so they should keep **$100,000 in cash reserves**. ### **7. Inflation & Life Expectancy Adjustments** - **Inflation** (historically **~3% annually**) reduces purchasing power over time. - **Life expectancy** (now **77+ years** for men, **81+ for women**) means savings must last **longer**. - *Example:* If you retire at **65**, you may need savings to last **25-30 years**—not just 20. ### **8. Tax Implications** - **Traditional IRA/401(k) withdrawals** are taxed as income. - **Roth IRA withdrawals** are tax-free. - **Capital gains taxes** on investments matter. *Example:* A retiree with **$1 million in a 401(k)** could face **$30,000+ in annual taxes** if they withdraw **$100,000/year** (depending on tax bracket). --- ## **
