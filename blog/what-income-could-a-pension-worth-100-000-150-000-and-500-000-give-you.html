How Much Income Could a £100,000, £150,000, or £500,000 Pension Give You? (A Complete Breakdown)

How Much Income Can Your Pension Generate?

Your pension’s income potential depends on three key factors:

  1. The size of your pension pot (£100k, £150k, £500k, etc.)
  2. The withdrawal strategy (annuity vs. drawdown)
  3. Market conditions & inflation

Below, we’ll explore realistic income scenarios for each pension size, using 2024 data from the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), Money Advice Service, and pension providers.

1. A £100,000 Pension: What Can You Expect?

A £100,000 pension is a solid start, but it’s not enough for a comfortable retirement unless you have other income sources (e.g., State Pension, part-time work, or savings).

Option 1: Pension Drawdown (Flexible Access)

If you take flexible access, you can withdraw money as needed, but tax rules apply:

  • Up to 25% tax-free (the first withdrawal).
  • Remaining 75% taxed as income (your marginal rate applies).

Example Income Scenarios (2024):

Withdrawal Rate Annual Income (Before Tax) After-Tax Income (Basic Rate) After-Tax Income (Higher Rate)
4% £4,000 £3,200 £2,400
5% £5,000 £3,750 £2,500
6% £6,000 £4,500 £2,700

Key Considerations:

  • The 25/75 rule means you can’t withdraw more than 25% tax-free in the first year.
  • Withdrawing too much too soon risks running out of money—especially if markets drop.
  • Inflation erodes purchasing power—a £4,000 income today may only buy £3,500 worth of goods in 10 years if inflation averages 1.5%.

Real-World Example: *John, 65, has a £100,000 pension and withdraws £4,000 per year (4%). After tax (basic rate), he gets £3,200. However, in Year 3, he needs to replace his car, so he takes an extra £5,000. Now, his pot drops to £91,000, and his remaining income is £3,640 (4% of £91k). If he lives to 90, he may run out of money before then.*

Best Strategy for £100k Pension: ✔ Withdraw 3-4% per year (adjusting for inflation). ✔ Consider a small annuity (e.g., £500/month) for guaranteed income. ✔ Delay State Pension if eligible (up to age 70) to boost monthly payments.

Option 2: Buying an Annuity

An annuity provides lifetime guaranteed income, but rates depend on age, health, and market conditions.

2024 Annuity Rates (Single Life, Age 65, £100k):

  • £5,200 per year (if healthy).
  • £5,800 per year (if smoker or with health conditions).

Pros: ✅ Guaranteed income for life—no risk of running out. ✅ No investment risk—pension provider bears the risk.

Cons: ❌ Lower income than drawdown if you die young. ❌ No flexibility—you can’t access extra cash if needed.

Real-World Example: Sarah, 66, buys a £5,200/year annuity with her £100k. She lives to 90, receiving £312,000 total—but if she dies at 75, she only gets £104,000. If she had kept it in drawdown, she could have left a legacy.

Best Strategy for Annuity: ✔ Compare rates (use MoneyHelper’s annuity calculator). ✔ Consider a "phased annuity" (e.g., £3,000 now + £2,000 later). ✔ Leave some money in drawdown for flexibility.

2. A £150,000 Pension: Can You Retire Comfortably?

A £150,000 pension is better but still requires careful planning. With State Pension (£221.20/week in 2024), you can live modestly, but luxury retirement is harder.

Option 1: Pension Drawdown (Flexible Access)

Withdrawal Rate Annual Income (Before Tax) After-Tax Income (Basic Rate) After-Tax Income (Higher Rate)
4% £6,000 £4,500 £3,600
5% £7,500 £5,625 £3,750
6% £9,000 £6,750 £4,500

Key Considerations:

  • Inflation protection is critical—£6,000 today may only buy £4,500 in 10 years.
  • Tax efficiency matters—if you’re in the higher rate (40%), your effective income drops significantly.

Real-World Example: *David, 67, has £150k and withdraws £7,500 (5%). After tax (basic rate), he gets £5,625. He also has a State Pension of £9,300/year, bringing his total to £14,925/year (~£1,244/month). This is comfortable but not luxurious—he can afford a small car, utilities, and some leisure, but holidays or big purchases require budgeting.*

Best Strategy for £150k Pension: ✔ Withdraw 4-5% per year, adjusting for inflation. ✔ Use the "4% rule" with a buffer—keep 20-30% in investments for market downturns. ✔ Consider a "hybrid approach" (e.g., £5,000 annuity + £2,500 drawdown).

Option 2: Buying an Annuity

2024 Annuity Rates (Single Life, Age 65, £150k):

  • £7,800 per year (healthy).
  • £8,700 per year (smoker/health conditions).

Pros: ✅ Guaranteed income for life—no investment risk. ✅ Higher income than drawdown if you have health issues.

Cons: ❌ Less flexibility—you can’t access extra cash. ❌ Waste of money if you die young (e.g., £7,800/year for 10 years = £78k spent, £72k left).

Real-World Example: Emma, 68, buys a £7,800/year annuity. She lives to 85, receiving £62,400 total. If she had kept it in drawdown, she could have £75k left for a legacy or extra spending.

Best Strategy for Annuity: ✔ Compare rates (use MoneyHelper’s annuity calculator). ✔ Consider a "joint life annuity" if married (lower income but covers spouse). ✔ Leave some money in drawdown for flexibility.

4% £20,000 £15,000 £12,000

6% £30,000 £22,500 £18,000

Key Considerations:

  • Inflation protection is critical—£20k today may only buy £15k in 10 years.
  • Tax efficiency matters—if you’re in the additional rate (45%), your effective income drops to £11,000.
  • Market downturns can hurt—a 20% drop in investments could reduce your pot by £100k.

Real-World Example: *Michael, 65, has £500k and withdraws £20,000 (4%). After tax (basic rate), he gets £15,000. He also has a State Pension of £9,300, bringing his total to £24,300/year (~£2,025/month). This allows for:

  • Mortgage-free home
  • Two holidays per year
  • Private healthcare
  • Discretionary spending (~£500/month)

However, if he withdraws £30,000 (6%), his pot drops to £470k after Year 1. If the market crashes, he could be forced to sell assets at a loss.

Best Strategy for £500k Pension: ✔ Withdraw 3-4% per year, adjusting for inflation. ✔ Use the "4% rule with a buffer"—keep 30-40% in investments for market downturns. ✔ Consider a "hybrid approach" (e.g., £15,000 annuity + £10,000 drawdown).

8 Actionable Strategies to Maximise Your Pension Income

Now that we’ve covered what income you can expect, let’s explore how to make the most of your pension—whether you have £100k, £150k, or £500k.

1. Use the "4% Rule" (With Adjustments for Inflation)

The "4% rule" (popularised by financial planner William Bengen) suggests withdrawing 4% of your pension pot annually, adjusting for inflation.

Why it works:

  • Historically, markets return ~7%, so 4% is sustainable over time.
  • Adjusting for inflation (e.g., +2% each year) ensures your money lasts longer.

How to apply it:

  • Year 1: Withdraw 4% (e.g., £4,000 from £100k).
  • Year 2: Withdraw 4.08% (4% + 0.8% inflation).
  • Repeat annually.

Real-World Example: A £500k pension following the 4% rule:

  • Year 1: £20,000
  • Year 2: £20,800
  • Year 3: £21,616
  • ...and so on.

This ensures your money lasts 30+ years without running out.
