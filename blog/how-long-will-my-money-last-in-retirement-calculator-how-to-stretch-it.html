If they wait until 70, their benefit jumps to **$2,250/month**—a **$900/month increase** that lasts for life.* **How to implement:** - Use the **Social Security Administration’s retirement estimator** ([ssa.gov](https://www.ssa.gov)) to see your break-even point. - If you’re in good health, **wait until 70** unless you need the income early. ### **Strategy 2: Downsize Your Home (Or Rent Instead of Own)** **Why it works:** - **Housing costs** are the **#1 expense** for retirees (averaging **25-30% of income**, per AARP). - Downsizing or renting can **free up $1,000-$3,000/month** to invest or spend elsewhere. **Real-World Example:** *A couple living in a $500,000 home with a $2,500/month mortgage downsizes to a $200,000 condo with a $1,000/month mortgage. They use the extra **$1,500/month** to pay off debt or invest in dividend stocks.* **How to implement:** - **Sell your primary home** and move to a **smaller, more affordable property**. - **Consider reverse mortgages** (if you own your home outright) to access equity without selling. - **Rent instead of buy** if you can find a **long-term lease** with lower costs. ### **Strategy 3: The "Reverse Mortgage" Strategy (For Homeowners 62+)** **Why it works:** - A **reverse mortgage** allows homeowners **62+** to borrow against their home equity **without making payments**. - The loan is **repaid when you sell, move out, or pass away**. - You keep **title to your home** and can live mortgage-free. **Real-World Example:** *A retiree with a $400,000 home and no mortgage takes out a **$200,000 reverse mortgage**. Even if they live to 95, their income is **guaranteed**—no market risk. **How to implement:** - **Shop multiple insurers** (rates vary by age/health). - **Consider a "joint and survivor" annuity** if both spouses need income. - **Leave some savings untouched** (annuities are **not liquid**). ### **Strategy 6: Work Part-Time or Consulting in Retirement** **Why it works:** - **Reduces withdrawal needs** (e.g., earning **$2,000/month** means you withdraw **$2,000 less** from savings). - **Keeps you mentally engaged** and socially connected. **Real-World Example:** *A retired engineer takes a **part-time consulting job** for **$3,000/month**. This **saved them $50,000+ in the first 5 years**. **How to implement:** - **Shop for Medicare plans** (compare **Part C (Advantage) vs. Medigap**). - **Use an HSA** (if eligible) for **tax-free medical expenses**. -
