  • Cost-of-Living Adjustments (COLAs): Some plans include annual inflation adjustments (usually 2–3% per year).

2. Supplemental Retirement Accounts (SRA) & 401(k) Plans

Common Mistakes Ecolab Retirees Make (And How to Avoid Them)

Even the most careful retirees can fall into pension calculation traps. Here are the most frequent errors and how to prevent them.

❌ Mistake 1: Assuming Your Pension is "Set in Stone"

Why It’s Dangerous:

  • Pension plans can be amended (e.g., COLAs reduced, vesting periods extended).
  • Company mergers may change benefit terms.

How to Avoid It:

  • Review your pension agreement annually.
  • Document all changes in writing.
  • Consult a lawyer before signing any new pension documents.

❌ Mistake 2: Not Comparing Your Pension to Similar Jobs

Why It’s Dangerous:

  • Some retirees don’t know if they’re getting a "fair" pension.
  • Industry standards vary (e.g., oil & gas workers often have better pensions than tech employees).

How to Avoid It:

  • Use pension calculators (e.g., PensionRights.org, AARP’s Retirement Calculator).
  • Talk to former colleagues in similar roles.
  • Hire a pension consultant to benchmark your benefits.

❌ Mistake 3: Ignoring Tax Implications of Pension Withdrawals

Why It’s Dangerous:

  • Lump sums are taxed as income (could push you into a higher tax bracket).
  • State taxes vary (some states tax pensions heavily, others don’t tax them at all).

How to Avoid It:

  • Run tax projections using TurboTax or H&R Block’s retirement tools.
  • Consult a CPA before taking a lump sum.
  • Consider moving to a low-tax state if pensions are a major income source.

❌ Mistake 4: Not Understanding Social Security Offsets

Why It’s Dangerous:

  • Ecolab’s pension may be reduced if you take early Social Security.
  • Windfall Elimination Provision (WEP) can cut your Social Security by 50% if you worked <30 years in covered employment.

How to Avoid It:

  • Use Social Security’s retirement estimator.
  • Delay Social Security until 70 to maximize benefits.
  • Consult a Social Security attorney if you’re affected by WEP.

❌ Mistake 5: Failing to Appeal Denied Claims

Why It’s Dangerous:

  • Ecolab’s pension department may make errors (e.g., wrong FAP, missed years of service).
  • Many retirees give up after one denial, even if they have strong evidence.

How to Avoid It:

  • File an appeal in writing (email or certified mail).
  • Hire a pension lawyer if the denial is unjustified.
  • Escalate to the PBGC if Ecolab’s plan is underfunded.
