How EPS Pensions Work: A Quick Overview

Before diving into the 2026 hike, let’s recap how EPS pensions are structured:

1. Who is Eligible for EPS?

  • Employees in organized sectors (private companies, government undertakings, etc.) who have PF contributions deducted.
  • Minimum service requirement: 10 years (though some states offer pensions after 5 years).
  • Salary cap: As of 2024, the maximum basic salary considered for EPS is ₹15,000 per month (for calculating pension).

2. How is EPS Pension Calculated?

The pension amount is determined using the following formula:

Monthly Pension = (Basic Salary × 15/80) × (Years of Service / 60)

  • Basic Salary: The first ₹15,000 of your salary (as per EPFO rules).
  • 15/80: A fixed multiplier (15% of basic salary).
  • Years of Service: Divided by 60 (to cap the maximum pension at ₹7,500 under current rules).

Example: If you earn ₹20,000/month and work for 20 years, your pension would be: (₹15,000 × 15/80) × (20/60) = ₹1,875/month

3. Current Minimum Pension: ₹9,000 vs. ₹3,000 Debate

The current minimum pension under EPS is ₹9,000 per month (for those with 20+ years of service). However, many retirees receive much less—sometimes as low as ₹1,000-₹2,000/month—due to:

  • Lower basic salary (if earnings were below ₹15,000)
  • Incomplete service years (due to job changes or breaks)
  • No pension for family members (unless they were dependent)

This has led to public demand for a ₹3,000 minimum pension, which could be introduced in 2026 as part of Atal Pension Yojana (APY) integration or EPFO reforms.

8 Actionable Strategies to Maximize Your EPS Pension Before 2026

Even if the 2026 hike is confirmed, you can take proactive steps now to secure the highest possible pension. Here’s how:

1. Ensure Continuous PF Contributions (No Gaps)

Problem: If you stop contributing to PF for more than 3 months, your service years may not count for pension. Solution:

  • Check your PF passbook every 3 months.
  • Request salary slips to confirm deductions.
  • Use the EPFO app to track contributions.

Real-World Example: A 40-year-old employee in Mumbai had a 3-month gap in PF contributions. When he retired, his pension was reduced by ₹200/month because those months were not counted.

2. Increase Your Basic Salary Before Retirement

Since only ₹15,000 (or soon ₹20,000) is considered for pension, earning more before retirement can boost your pension. How?

  • Ask for a salary hike (if eligible).
  • Switch to a higher-paying job (if possible).
  • Get promotions to increase basic pay.

Example: A software engineer in Bangalore negotiated a ₹25,000 basic salary before retirement. His pension jumped from ₹2,200 to ₹3,000 (under current rules).

3. Claim Pension Early (If Eligible for 5-Year Rule)

Most states require 10 years of service for EPS pension, but some allow 5 years with lower benefits. Action:

  • Check your state’s EPS rules (e.g., Maharashtra, Gujarat, and Karnataka offer pensions after 5 years).
  • Apply early if you meet the criteria.

Warning:

  • Pension after 5 years is lower (often ₹500-₹1,000/month).
  • Best for short-term retirees (e.g., those who quit early).

4. Opt for Family Pension (If Applicable)

Currently, only ₹15,000 of basic salary is considered for family pension. If this increases to ₹20,000+, your dependent family members may get a higher pension. How to Claim?

  • Submit death certificate + family details to EPFO.
  • Ensure you were a dependent (spouse, children under 25).

5. Use the EPFO Online Portal for Claims

Mistake: Many retirees miss out on pension because they don’t file claims properly. Solution:

  • Visit EPFO’s official website.
  • Submit Form 10D (Pension Claim Form) at least 3 months before retirement.
  • Track status online to avoid delays.

Example: A retired bank employee in Delhi forgot to submit Form 10D. His pension was delayed by 6 months, costing him ₹18,000.

6. Check for Backdated Pension Claims

If you missed contributions due to job changes or company closures, you may be eligible for backdated pension. How?

  • File a grievance on the EPFO portal.
  • Provide proof of service (appointment letters, salary slips).

7. Consider Voluntary Contributions (If Eligible)

Some employees can voluntarily contribute to increase their pension. How?

  • Check with your employer if they offer voluntary EPS contributions.
  • This can boost your pension beyond the ₹7,500 cap.

8. Plan for Post-Retirement Income (If Pension is Low)

If the 2026 hike doesn’t cover you, consider:

  • Switching to Atal Pension Yojana (APY) (if eligible).
  • Investing in PPF or NPS for extra income.
  • Taking a loan against PF (if needed).

Common Mistakes That Reduce Your EPS Pension (And How to Avoid Them)

Many retirees unintentionally lose out on higher pensions due to these common mistakes:

1. Not Updating PF Details After Job Change

Mistake: Changing jobs but not transferring PF balance to the new employer. Solution:

  • Use the EPFO portal to transfer PF online.
  • Submit Form 13 to your new employer.

2. Assuming Higher Salary Doesn’t Affect Pension

Mistake: Earning ₹30,000/month but only ₹15,000 (or soon ₹20,000) counts for pension. Solution:

  • Negotiate a higher basic salary (not just DA or HRA).
  • Check if your company allows voluntary EPS contributions.

3. Not Claiming Pension on Time

Mistake: Filing Form 10D too late, leading to delays or rejection. Solution:

  • Submit claims at least 3 months before retirement.
  • Follow up online if there’s no response.

4. Ignoring State-Specific EPS Rules

Mistake: Assuming 10 years of service is universal, but some states offer pensions after 5 years. Solution:

  • Check your state’s EPS rules (e.g., Maharashtra, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu have different norms).
  • Apply early if eligible.

5. Not Considering Family Pension

Mistake: Not claiming family pension because it’s "extra effort." Solution:

  • Submit death certificate + family details to EPFO.
  • Ensure dependent family members are listed.

6. Assuming PF Balance is Safe (It’s Not!)

Mistake: Leaving PF untouched, assuming it’s "safe." Solution:

  • Withdraw only emergency funds (PF is for retirement).
  • Use the EPFO calculator to project future pension.

7. Not Updating Aadhaar-Linkage

Mistake: EPFO cannot process claims if Aadhaar is not linked. Solution:

  • Link Aadhaar to PF account via the EPFO portal.
