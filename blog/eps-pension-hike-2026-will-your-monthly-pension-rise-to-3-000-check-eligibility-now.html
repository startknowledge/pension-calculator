Understanding EPS Pension: How It Works & What’s Changing in 2026

1. What is the Employees’ Pension Scheme (EPS)?

The EPS is a defined benefit pension scheme under the EPFO, where employees contribute 8.33% of their basic salary (up to ₹15,000 in 2024) towards their pension fund. The employer also contributes the same amount, making the total contribution 16.66% of the basic salary.

  • Minimum pension: ₹1,000 per month (for those with lower salaries)
  • Maximum pension: ₹7,500 per month (as per current rules)
  • Pensionable salary cap: ₹15,000 (as of 2024)

2. How is EPS Pension Calculated?

The current EPS pension formula is: Monthly Pension = (Basic Salary × 1/70) × Pensionable Service

  • Basic Salary: The salary on which EPF and EPS contributions are made (capped at ₹15,000).
  • Pensionable Service: The number of years you’ve worked under EPS (minimum 10 years required for full pension).

Example: If your basic salary is ₹15,000 and you’ve worked for 20 years, your pension would be: (₹15,000 × 1/70) × 20 = ₹4,285.71 per month

However, this formula is being revised to ensure higher pensions for retirees.

3. The Proposed EPS Pension Hike for 2026

The Government has been discussing a major reform to increase the pension amount for EPS subscribers. Key changes expected in 2026 include:

✔ Higher pension multiplier (from 1/70 to 1/50 or 1/60) – This would double or triple the pension amount. ✔ Removal of the ₹15,000 salary cap – More employees will qualify for higher pensions. ✔ Indexation of pensions with inflation – Future pension increases will adjust for rising prices. ✔ Minimum pension guarantee of ₹3,000+ – For those with 15+ years of service.

Current vs. Proposed Pension Calculation (Example):

Current (1/70 Formula) Proposed (1/50 Formula)
₹15,000 × (1/70) × 20 = ₹4,285.71 ₹15,000 × (1/50) × 20 = ₹6,000

This means your pension could jump from ₹2,000 to ₹6,000+ if the 1/50 multiplier is implemented.

Will Your Pension Really Reach ₹3,000 in 2026? Check Eligibility Now

Not everyone will automatically get a ₹3,000+ pension in 2026. Eligibility depends on: ✅ Your service years (minimum 10 years, but 15+ years for higher benefits) ✅ Your basic salary (higher salary = higher pension) ✅ Whether you’re a new or existing subscriber (some reforms may not apply retroactively) ✅ Your employer’s compliance (some companies may not contribute fully)

How to Check Your EPS Pension Eligibility for ₹3,000+

Step 1: Log in to Your EPFO Member Portal

  • Visit https://www.epfindia.gov.in
  • Enter your UAN (Universal Account Number) and password
  • Go to "Member Passbook" to see your service history and contributions

Step 2: Verify Your Pensionable Service

  • If you have 15+ years of service, you’re closer to ₹3,000+
  • If you have less than 10 years, you may not qualify for a pension (but can withdraw PF)

Step 3: Check Your Basic Salary

  • If your basic salary is ₹15,000+, you’ll get the maximum pension under current rules
  • If it’s below ₹15,000, your pension will be proportional

Step 4: Use the EPS Pension Calculator

  • The EPFO provides an online calculator at https://www.epfindia.gov.in/site_en/Pension_Calculator.php
  • Enter your basic salary, service years, and date of birth to get an estimated pension

8 Actionable Strategies to Ensure Your EPS Pension Reaches ₹3,000+ in 2026

If you want to maximize your pension, follow these proven strategies:

1. Contribute to EPS for the Maximum Possible Years

  • Minimum 10 years required for a pension, but 15+ years ensures higher benefits.
  • If you’re close to retirement, stay employed even if you switch jobs (as long as your new employer is registered under EPFO).

Real-World Example: A 45-year-old employee with 12 years of service currently gets ₹1,800/month. If they work for 5 more years, their pension could jump to ₹3,000+ under the new formula.

2. Ensure Your Employer is Fully Compliant with EPFO

  • Some companies don’t contribute the full 8.33% towards EPS.
  • Check your EPF passbook – If your employer’s contribution is missing, demand a correction.
  • If your employer doesn’t contribute, you won’t get a full pension.

How to Fix It:

  • File a grievance on the EPFO portal
  • Send a formal complaint to your HR
  • If unresolved, approach the EPFO regional office

3. Avoid Breaks in Service (If Possible)

  • Gaps in service reduce your pensionable years.
  • If you leave a job for more than 3 months, your service may not be counted for EPS.
  • Solution: If you’re between jobs, consider taking a short leave instead of quitting.

4. Opt for the New EPS Pension Formula (If Available)

  • The Government is testing a new formula (1/50 or 1/60 multiplier).
  • New subscribers (post-2024) may get this automatically.
  • Existing subscribers may need to apply for the upgrade.

How to Apply:

  • Check for EPFO notifications in 2025-2026
  • Submit a request via the EPFO portal if the option is available

5. Increase Your Basic Salary (If Possible)

  • Your pension depends on your basic salary (capped at ₹15,000).
  • If you’re eligible for a salary hike, negotiate for a raise before retirement.
  • Promotions or skill upgrades can boost your basic pay.

Example: An employee with ₹12,000 basic salary gets ₹2,571/month pension. If they get a ₹3,000 raise, their pension could increase by ₹434/month.

6. Claim Family Pension (If Eligible)

  • If you die before retirement, your spouse or dependent children can get a family pension.
  • Minimum family pension: ₹1,000/month (varies by state).
  • Maximum family pension: Up to 50% of your pension.

How to Claim:

  • Submit death certificate + family details to EPFO
  • The family member must be registered as a nominee

7. Use the EPS Withdrawal Option Wisely (If Needed)

  • If you leave service before 10 years, you can withdraw PF + EPS.
  • But: This reduces future pension benefits.
  • Best option: Stay until retirement to maximize pension.

8. Keep Track of EPFO Announcements for 2026

  • The Government may announce new rules in 2025-2026.
  • Subscribe to EPFO’s official newsletter or follow Pension-Calculator’s updates.
  • Join EPFO-related Facebook groups for real-time updates.

Common Mistakes That Reduce Your EPS Pension (And How to Avoid Them)

Many employees unintentionally lose out on higher pensions due to these mistakes:

❌ Mistake 1: Not Updating Your UAN & Bank Details

  • If your UAN is inactive, your pension may not be credited.
  • Solution: Verify UAN monthly and update bank details in the EPFO portal.

❌ Mistake 2: Switching Jobs Without Transferring PF

  • If you don’t transfer PF when changing jobs, service years may not add up.
  • Solution: Use the "PF Transfer Claim" form within 30 days of new job.

❌ Mistake 3: Not Claiming Pension on Time

  • If you don’t apply for pension after retirement, you lose benefits.
  • Solution: Submit Form 10D 3 months before retirement.

❌ Mistake 4: Assuming All Employers Contribute to EPS

  • Some small companies or contractors may not contribute.
  • Solution: Check your EPF passbook for employer contributions.

❌ Mistake 5: Not Opting for Indexation Benefits

  • Inflation adjustment is not automatic in some cases.
  • Solution: Request indexation when filing your pension claim.

❌ Mistake 6: Not Checking for Overlapping Pension Schemes

  • If you’re covered under both EPS and NPS, you may double-count benefits.
  • Solution: Consult EPFO to avoid pension overlaps.
