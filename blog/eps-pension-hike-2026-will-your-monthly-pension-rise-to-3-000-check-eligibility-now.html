Understanding the EPS Pension Hike 2026: What’s Changing?

1. What is the Employees’ Pension Scheme (EPS)?

The EPS is a defined benefit pension scheme under the EPF Act, 1952, where employees contribute 8.33% of their basic salary, and employers match this contribution. The pension amount is calculated based on:

  • Salary (basic + dearness allowance, if applicable)
  • Years of service
  • Current pension formula (as per EPS 1995 rules)

2. How is the Pension Amount Calculated?

The current EPS pension formula is: Monthly Pension = (Basic Salary × 1/70) × Years of Service

For example:

  • If your basic salary is ₹15,000 and you’ve worked for 20 years, your pension would be: ₹15,000 × (1/70) × 20 = ₹4,285.71 per month

However, most workers receive far less because:

  • Many earn below ₹15,000 basic salary
  • Some have less than 20 years of service
  • The ₹3,000 cap (introduced in 2014) limits higher pensions

3. The ₹3,000 Pension Cap: Why It’s Being Revisited

In 2014, the government introduced a ₹3,000 cap on EPS pensions to control expenditure. However, with rising inflation and wage hikes, many argue that this cap is outdated.

The Budget 2024 hinted at revising pension formulas, and EPS 2026 reforms may: ✔ Remove or adjust the ₹3,000 cap ✔ Increase the pension multiplier (1/70 → 1/60 or better) ✔ Allow higher contributions from employers

Will your pension reach ₹3,000+? It depends on your salary, service years, and eligibility under new rules.

Common Mistakes That Reduce Your EPS Pension (And How to Avoid Them)

Mistake 1: Not Transferring PF When Changing Jobs

  • Problem: If you don’t transfer PF, your service years reset, reducing pension eligibility.
  • Solution: Use the EPFO UAN transfer portal to merge accounts.

Mistake 2: Withdrawing PF Before Retirement

  • Problem: Partial withdrawals reduce your pension amount.
  • Solution: Only withdraw after retirement or for serious emergencies.

Mistake 3: Not Claiming Pension on Time

  • Problem: Delayed claims can lead to penalties or reduced payouts.
  • Solution: File claims 6 months before retirement.

Mistake 4: Underreporting Salary

  • Problem: If your basic salary is lower than actual, your pension will be less.
  • Solution: Verify salary records and request corrections.

Mistake 5: Ignoring State-Specific Pension Schemes

  • Problem: Many states offer additional pension benefits that workers miss.
  • Solution: Check with your state labor department for extra schemes.
