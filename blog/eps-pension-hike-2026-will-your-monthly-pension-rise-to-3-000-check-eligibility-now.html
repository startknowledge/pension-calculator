Understanding EPS: How It Works & Why a Hike is Needed

What is the Employees’ Pension Scheme (EPS)?

EPS is a defined benefit pension scheme administered by the EPFO, offering retirement benefits to employees of organized sectors. Key features include:

  • Mandatory for employers with 20+ employees (under the Employees’ Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952).
  • Employee contribution: 8.33% of basic salary + dearness allowance (DA) (capped at ₹15,000/month as of 2024).
  • Employer contribution: 8.33% of basic + DA (matched by the employee).
  • Pension amount: Calculated as 9% of average salary (last 12 months) × years of service (capped at 35 years).

Why is a Pension Hike Needed?

Despite EPS being a social security net, its current structure has major drawbacks:

  1. Low Pension Amounts – Many retirees receive less than ₹1,000/month, which is insufficient for survival.
  2. Inflation Erosion – The ₹1,000–₹2,000 range today may not cover ₹3,000+ expenses in 2026.
  3. Incomplete Coverage – Only 20% of India’s workforce is under EPS (per EPFO 2023 report), leaving millions unprotected.
  4. Fixed Contribution Cap – The ₹15,000 salary cap limits pension growth for higher-paid employees.

Government’s Stance on EPS Reforms

The Union Budget 2024 announced:

  • Increased pension for low-income workers (targeting ₹3,000/month for eligible retirees).
  • Digitalization of claims to reduce delays.
  • Higher employer contributions in some sectors to boost pension funds.

However, no official announcement has been made for a 2026 hike. But with rising political pressure and public demand, a ₹3,000 pension target is being discussed.

The ₹3,000 Pension Target: How It Could Happen

The ₹3,000/month pension is part of a larger social security push, including:

  3. Extended Service Years – Allowing beyond 35 years of service for higher pensions.
  4. Portability & Merging Accounts – If you switch jobs, your EPS contributions can be merged for a better pension.

To reach ₹3,000:

8 Actionable Strategies to Maximize Your EPS Pension (Even Before 2026)

1. Check Your EPS Account Statement & Claim Missing Contributions

Problem: Many workers don’t realize they missed contributions due to job changes or employer errors.

Solution:

  • Log in to EPFO’s online portal (www.epfindia.gov.in) and check your UAN (Universal Account Number) statement.
  • File a claim for missing contributions if any years are missing.
  • Use the "Member Passbook" feature to track contributions.

Real-World Example: Priya, a bank employee, found out she missed 3 years of EPS contributions due to a bank merger. After filing a claim, she recovered ₹45,000 in unclaimed pension credits, boosting her future pension by ₹1,200/month.

2. Ensure Continuous Contributions (No Gaps)

Problem: A single year of no contribution can slash your pension by ₹1,000–₹2,000.

Solution:

  • Switch jobs smoothly – If you leave a job, transfer your EPS balance to the new employer.
  • Inform EPFO of salary changes – If your salary increases, update your UAN to avoid capping.
  • Check for "Non-Contributory Periods" – If you were temporarily unemployed, request a waiver for those years.

3. Opt for Portability of EPS (If You Switch Jobs)

Problem: If you change jobs frequently, your EPS contributions may not be merged, reducing your pension.

Solution:

  • Request EPS portability when switching jobs.
  • Use the "Transfer Claim" option in the EPFO portal.
  • Keep records of all EPS accounts (some employers don’t auto-transfer).

Real-World Example: Amit, a software engineer, worked in 3 different companies before retiring. By merging his EPS accounts, he increased his pension from ₹1,800 to ₹2,800/month.

4. Increase Your Contributory Salary (If Below ₹15,000)

Problem: The ₹15,000 salary cap limits pension growth for lower-paid workers.

Solution:

  • Negotiate a salary hike (if your basic + DA exceeds ₹15,000).
  • Claim DA separately – Some employers exclude DA from EPS, reducing pension.
  • Check for "Revised Salary Structure" – If your basic salary was increased, update your UAN.

5. Claim Pension Early (If Eligible)

Problem: Many workers wait too long to claim pension, missing out on inflation-adjusted increases.

Solution:

  • Retire at 58–60 years (instead of waiting until 62).
  • Check for "Early Pension" options if you have 10+ years of service.
  • File a pension claim before turning 60 to avoid delays.

6. Use the "Family Pension" Option for Dependents

Problem: If you die before retirement, your family may not get full benefits.

Solution:

  • Ensure your nominee is updated in the EPFO portal.
  • Check for "Family Pension" eligibility (if you die before 60, your spouse/children may get 50–100% of your pension).
  • File a claim within 2 years of death to avoid penalties.

7. Consider Voluntary Contributions (If Employer Allows)

Problem: Some employers don’t contribute enough, reducing pension.

Solution:

  • Ask your employer for "Voluntary EPS Contributions" (if allowed).
  • Some private companies offer "Superannuation Benefits" (extra pension beyond EPS).
  • Invest in PPF or NPS to supplement your EPS income.

8. Stay Updated with Government Announcements

Problem: Many workers miss pension hike notifications from the government.

Solution:

  • Subscribe to EPFO’s official newsletter (www.epfindia.gov.in).
  • Follow labor ministry updates (Twitter, official website).
  • Join pension-related forums (e.g., EPFO WhatsApp groups).

Common Mistakes That Could Cost You Extra Pension (And How to Avoid Them)

Mistake 1: Not Updating Your UAN After a Job Change

Why it’s bad: If you don’t update your UAN, your new salary may not be considered for pension calculations.

Solution:

  • Link your Aadhaar, PAN, and bank details to your UAN.
  • Verify your salary details in the EPFO portal every 6 months.

Mistake 2: Ignoring the "Revised Salary Structure"

Why it’s bad: If your basic salary increases, but you don’t update EPS, your pension stays capped at ₹15,000.

Solution:

  • Check your "Salary Certificate" from HR.
  • File a "Salary Revision Claim" in the EPFO portal.

Mistake 3: Not Claiming Missing Contributions

Why it’s bad: Unclaimed contributions can reduce your pension by ₹500–₹2,000/month.

Solution:

  • Run a "Contribution History Report" in the EPFO portal.
  • File a claim within 5 years of the missing contribution.

Mistake 4: Waiting Too Long to Claim Pension

Why it’s bad: If you delay claiming pension, you miss inflation adjustments.

Solution:

  • File a pension claim at 58–60 years (instead of waiting until 62).
  • Use the "Online Pension Claim" option to avoid delays.

Mistake 5: Not Nominating a Family Member for Family Pension

Why it’s bad: If you die before retirement, your family may get nothing if no nominee is listed.

Solution:

  • Update your nominee in the EPFO portal.
  • Provide Aadhaar details of your nominee for faster claims.

Mistake 6: Assuming EPS is the Only Pension Source

Why it’s bad: Many workers don’t explore other pension options like NPS or PPF.

Solution:

  • Contribute to NPS (National Pension Scheme) for tax benefits.
  • Invest in PPF (Public Provident Fund) for extra retirement savings.
