EPS Pension Hike 2026: Will Your Monthly Pension Rise to ₹3,000? Check Eligibility Now

Understanding the EPS Pension Hike 2026: What’s Changing?

The Employees’ Pension Scheme (EPS) is governed by the Employees’ Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) and is linked to the Employees’ Provident Fund (EPF). The minimum pension under EPS-95 was ₹1,000 per month, but recent amendments aim to increase this significantly.

Key Changes in EPS Pension Rules (2024-2026)

  1. Minimum Pension Increase

    • The minimum guaranteed pension under EPS-95 has been raised from ₹1,000 to ₹1,500 (as per Budget 2024).
    • Further hikes are expected by 2026, with some reports suggesting a target of ₹3,000 per month for eligible retirees.

  2. EPS-95 (Amended) Scheme

    • The amended EPS-95 rules allow for higher pensions based on salary contributions.
    • Employees contributing more than ₹15,000/month (basic + DA) may see larger pension increases.

  3. Indexation of Pensions

    • Pensions are now indexed with inflation, meaning future increases are automatic based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI).

  4. Portability & Merging of Accounts

    • If you’ve worked in multiple companies, your EPS accounts can now be merged, potentially boosting your pension.

Who Is Eligible for the ₹3,000 EPS Pension Hike?

Not everyone will automatically get a ₹3,000 pension. Your eligibility depends on several factors:

1. Minimum Service Period

  • You must have at least 10 years of service under EPS to qualify for a pension.
  • Example: If you retired in 2023 with 12 years of service, you’re eligible for a minimum pension of ₹1,500 (current rate).

2. Salary Contribution Threshold

  • Employees earning ₹15,000/month or more (basic + DA) get higher pension contributions (8.33% of salary).
  • Lower earners (below ₹15,000) contribute 6.5%, which may limit their pension hike.

3. Age of Retirement

  • Early retirement (before 50) may reduce your pension.
  • Retiring at 58-60 (standard retirement age) ensures full pension benefits.

4. EPS-95 vs. EPS-95 (Amended) Scheme

  • Old EPS-95: Fixed minimum pension of ₹1,000.
  • New EPS-95 (Amended): Dynamic pension based on salary contributions—higher earnings = higher pension.

5. No Claim of Withdrawal Before Retirement

  • If you withdrew your PF before retirement, your pension may be reduced or denied.

How to Check If You’re Eligible for the ₹3,000 Pension Boost

Before assuming you’ll get a ₹3,000 pension, verify your eligibility with these steps:

Step 1: Log In to Your EPFO Member Portal

  • Visit https://www.epfindia.gov.in
  • Use your UAN (Universal Account Number) and password to log in.
  • Go to "Member Passbook" to check your PF and EPS contributions.

Step 2: Verify Your Service Years

  • Check "Service History" to confirm how many years you’ve contributed to EPS.
  • Minimum 10 years required for a pension.

Step 3: Check Your Salary Contribution Rate

  • If your basic + DA is ₹15,000 or more, you contribute 8.33% (higher pension).
  • If below ₹15,000, you contribute 6.5% (lower pension).

Step 4: Ensure No PF Withdrawals Before Retirement

  • If you withdrew PF before retirement, your pension may be reduced.
  • Solution: If you’ve already withdrawn, check if re-depositing is possible (consult EPFO).

Step 5: Apply for Pension Merging (If You Worked in Multiple Companies)

  • If you’ve worked in different firms, your EPS accounts may be separate.
  • Request a merger via the EPFO portal to combine contributions for a higher pension.

Step 6: Submit Form 10Q for Pension Claim

  • After retirement, submit Form 10Q to claim your pension.
  • Deadline: Within 30 days of retirement (late submissions may face delays).

8 Actionable Strategies to Maximize Your EPS Pension by 2026

If you want to ensure your pension reaches ₹3,000 (or more), follow these proven strategies:

1. Contribute More Than the Minimum (If Possible)

  • Employees earning ₹15,000+ get higher pension contributions (8.33%).
  • Action: If your salary is above ₹15,000, ensure full contributions are made.

2. Avoid PF Withdrawals Before Retirement

  • Every withdrawal reduces your pension.
  • Example: If you withdrew ₹50,000 from PF, your monthly pension may drop by ₹50–₹100.

3. Merge Multiple EPS Accounts (If You Worked in Different Companies)

  • Problem: Many workers have separate EPS accounts from different jobs.
  • Solution: Use the EPFO portal to merge accounts before retirement.

4. Opt for the EPS-95 (Amended) Scheme Instead of Old EPS-95

  • Old EPS-95: Fixed ₹1,000 minimum pension.
  • New EPS-95 (Amended): Dynamic pension based on salary.
  • Action: If you’re re-joining EPS, choose the amended scheme.

5. Increase Your Salary (If Possible) Before Retirement

  • Higher salary = higher pension contributions.
  • Example: If you earn ₹20,000 instead of ₹15,000, your pension contribution increases by ₹333/month.

6. Claim Indexation Benefits for Higher Pensions

  • Pensions are now indexed with inflation (CPI).
  • Action: Ensure your pension is adjusted annually to keep up with inflation.

7. Submit Form 10Q on Time (Within 30 Days of Retirement)

  • Late submissions can delay your pension.
  • Example: If you submit Form 10Q after 6 months, EPFO may reduce your pension.

8. Check for Government Subsidies & Additional Benefits

  • Some states offer additional pension benefits (e.g., Maharashtra’s ₹5,000 pension for low-income retirees).
  • Action: Research state-specific pension schemes to boost your income.

  • Solution: Switch to the new scheme if re-joining EPS.

4. Failing to Merge Multiple EPS Accounts

  • Problem: Separate accounts mean lower total pension.
  • Solution: Request a merger via the EPFO portal.

FAQs About EPS Pension Hike 2026

1. Will my EPS pension definitely rise to ₹3,000 by 2026?

No, it depends on your eligibility.

  • Minimum pension is now ₹1,500 (2024).
  • To reach ₹3,000, you need:
    • At least 10 years of service
    • Salary of ₹15,000+ (for higher contributions)
    • No PF withdrawals before retirement
    • Merged EPS accounts (if applicable)

Answer: Check your EPFO passbook and service history to confirm eligibility.

2. How can I check my EPS pension eligibility online?

You can verify your eligibility by:

  1. Logging in to EPFO Member Portal
  2. Checking "Service History" for total years contributed
  3. Confirming "PF Contribution Rate" (6.5% or 8.33%)
  4. Ensuring "No PF withdrawals before retirement"

Answer: Use the EPFO portal to cross-verify all details.

3. What happens if I withdraw my PF before retirement?

Withdrawing PF reduces your pension because:

  • PF funds are used to calculate pension.
  • Example: If you withdrew ₹2,00,000, your monthly pension may drop by ₹200–₹300.

Answer: Avoid PF withdrawals before retirement to maximize pension.

4. Can I merge my EPS accounts from different jobs?

Yes, you can merge multiple EPS accounts if:

  • You have separate UANs from different employers.
  • You retire from one job and join another under EPS.

How to merge?

  1. Log in to EPFO Member Portal
  2. Go to "Member Passbook"
  3. Request account merging under "Services"

Answer: Yes, merging can increase your total pension.

5. Will my EPS pension be adjusted for inflation?

Yes, pensions are now indexed with inflation (CPI) since 2024.

  • Old pensions (pre-2024) may not be fully indexed.
  • New pensions (post-2024) will get automatic adjustments.

Answer: Yes, future pensions will rise with inflation.
