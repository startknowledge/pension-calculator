1. Understanding the Congressional Pension System (CPS)

Before we dive into MTG’s move, it’s essential to grasp how the Congressional Pension System (CPS) works. Unlike private-sector pensions, which are often defined-contribution (401k-style), Congress operates under a defined-benefit system, meaning benefits are guaranteed based on years of service and salary.

Key components of the CPS:

  • Vesting Period: Employees must serve at least 5 years to qualify for any pension.
  • Full Pension Eligibility: After 20 years of service, members receive 100% of their pension.
  • Early Retirement Penalties: Leaving before age 62 (or 20 years of service, whichever comes first) can reduce benefits by up to 5% per year.
  • Lump-Sum vs. Monthly Payments: Members can choose between a one-time payout (taxed as income) or lifetime annuity payments.
