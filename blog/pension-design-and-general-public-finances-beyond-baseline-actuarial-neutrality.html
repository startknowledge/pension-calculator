Pension Design and General Public Finances: Beyond Baseline Actuarial Neutrality

In 2020, the global pension industry was valued at USD 1.9 trillion, and it's projected to grow to USD 2.6 trillion by 2025 (1). However, despite the growth of the pension industry, many pension plans and retirement systems face significant challenges, including funding shortfalls, low returns on investment, and an aging workforce.

Actuarial neutrality remains a fundamental principle in pension design, referring to the balance between the assets and liabilities of a pension system. However, baseline actuarial neutrality may not always be sufficient to ensure the long-term sustainability of pension systems. In this blog post, we will explore pension design and general public finances beyond baseline actuarial neutrality, providing actionable strategies, real-world examples, and expert insights to help policymakers, pension fiduciaries, and individuals make informed decisions about retirement planning.

1. Focus on Long-Term Sustainability

Pension systems often face immediate fiscal pressures, but neglecting long-term sustainability can lead to an even greater crisis down the road. (2) To address this challenge, policymakers and pension fiduciaries should focus on strategies that promote long-term sustainability, such as:

  • Investing in assets with a high return potential: Pension systems can invest in assets with a high return potential, such as private equity, real estate, or infrastructure.
  • Managing inflation risk: Pension systems can manage inflation risk through asset allocation, indexing benefits to inflation, or employing a long-term growth strategy.

2. Increase Fuel For The Fire

Most pension funds face a significant deficit, which needs to be fueled to make them inflation-indexed and progressive in design. The research says that we must allocate more resources to the pension fund to deal with this.

  • Increase funding rates: Pension funds can increase funding rates by allocating a higher percentage of employer and employee contributions towards the pension fund.
  • Consider payroll taxed contributions: Some countries, likeJapan, also use payroll taxed contributions to support their pension funds.

3. Emphasize Income Generation

Traditional pension systems are often designed to provide a steady income stream to beneficiaries. However, many pension systems face challenges in generating sufficient income to meet their liabilities. (3) To address this challenge, pension fiduciaries can emphasize income generation through:

  • Investing in dividend-paying stocks: Pension systems can invest in dividend-paying stocks to generate income and manage risk.
  • Asset-based income generation: Pension systems can employ asset-based income generation strategies, such as yield optimization or dividend reinvestment.

4. Focus on Long-Term Care and Wellness

Pension systems can provide support for long-term care and wellness, promoting healthy aging and reducing costs for healthcare. (4)

  • Offering wellness programs: Pension systems can offer wellness programs, such as health and fitness activities, mental health support, or financial literacy education.
  • Providing long-term care benefits: Pension systems can provide long-term care benefits, such as home care assistance, adult day care, or respite care.

5. Encouraging Deferred Compensation

Pension systems can adopt deferred compensation strategies, such as salary deferral programs or profit-sharing plans, to enhance retirement savings. (5)

  • Implementing employer matching programs: Pension systems can implement employer matching programs to incentivize employees to save for retirement.
  • Offering flexible compensation arrangements: Pension systems can offer flexible compensation arrangements, such as lump-sum payments or phantom stock options.

6. Invest in Pension System's Data Infrastructure

Pension systems require accurate, reliable, and robust data to inform decision-making and ensure the long-term sustainability of pension plans. (6) Investing in the pension system's data infrastructure can help to:

  • Improve data collection and analysis: Pension fiduciaries can invest in data collection and analysis tools to improve data quality and accuracy.
  • Enhance risk management capabilities: Pension fiduciaries can enhance risk management capabilities through the use of advanced data analytics.

7. Use Private Sector Solutions

Private sector solutions can provide innovative and cost-effective solutions for pension system reform. (7)

  • Contracting out non-core functions: Pension systems can contract out non-core functions, such as record-keeping or transfer agency services.
  • Collaborating with private sector providers: Pension systems can collaborate with private sector providers to access new pension designs, investment strategies, or risk management tools.

8. Increase Biodiversity for Sustainable Retirement

Creating a diverse portfolio that contains a combination of assets like stocks, bonds, real estate, and commodities, to create sustainable retirement income streams. To maximize this, we need to invest in a diverse portfolio with a mix of asset classes and achieve the set expected return to create sustainable retirement income streams.

  • Competitive, better-than-market returns: With the growing portfolio, such portfolio’s returns should compete positively with the market returns to ensure that there is sufficient returns on investments.
