Why Octopus Energy Is Spinning Out Kraken: The Strategic Rationale

Octopus Energy Group’s decision to separate Kraken is far from arbitrary. Several key factors are driving this move:

1. Unlocking Shareholder Value Through Specialization

Octopus Energy has grown rapidly over the past decade, expanding from a UK-focused renewable energy provider into a global player with interests in solar, wind, battery storage, and energy trading. However, its diverse portfolio—spanning retail energy, corporate energy solutions, and flexible energy services—has made it a jack-of-all-trades but master of none.

By spinning out Kraken, Octopus Energy aims to:

  • Focus on its core retail and corporate energy business while allowing Kraken to operate as an independent, specialized player in the flexible energy market.
  • Unlock shareholder value by creating a standalone entity with a clear valuation, making it easier for investors to assess Kraken’s potential independently.

Recent data supports this strategy:

  • The global energy storage market was valued at $10.7 billion in 2023 and is projected to grow at a CAGR of 18.5% through 2030 (BloombergNEF, 2023).
  • Battery storage adoption has surged, with over 20 GW of new capacity added globally in 2023 alone (IRENA, 2024).
  • Demand response and flexible energy services are becoming critical as grids struggle to balance supply and demand in the transition to renewables.

Kraken’s $8.65 billion valuation reflects its strong position in these high-growth segments, making it an attractive standalone entity.

2. Aligning with Regulatory and Market Trends

The energy sector is rapidly evolving due to:

  • Net-zero commitments from governments worldwide.
  • Increasing reliance on intermittent renewable energy sources (solar, wind) that require battery storage and demand response to maintain grid stability.
  • New regulatory frameworks (e.g., UK’s Smart Energy GB initiative, EU’s Clean Energy Package) that incentivize flexible energy solutions.

By spinning out Kraken, Octopus Energy can:

  • Dedicate full resources to scaling flexible energy solutions without being constrained by its broader corporate structure.
  • Attract institutional investors who are increasingly focusing on high-growth, niche energy sectors.

3. Capitalizing on Kraken’s Unique Position in Flexible Energy

Kraken is not just another battery storage company—it is a full-stack flexible energy provider, offering:

  • Battery storage solutions (both behind-the-meter and grid-scale).
  • Demand response services (helping businesses reduce costs by adjusting energy usage).
  • Energy trading platforms that optimize renewable energy deployment.
  • Aggregation services that connect multiple energy assets for better market participation.

Why is this valuable?

  • Grid stability is a growing concern as renewables dominate electricity generation. Kraken’s demand response and storage solutions help mitigate this issue.
  • Corporate energy buyers (e.g., tech giants, manufacturing firms) are actively seeking flexible energy solutions to reduce costs and meet sustainability goals.
  • Governments and utilities are investing heavily in energy flexibility to support the transition to net-zero.

Example: In 2023, National Grid ESO (UK) awarded Kraken a £100 million contract to provide demand flexibility services, demonstrating the commercial viability of its model.

4. Preparing for Future Mergers & Acquisitions (M&A)

Octopus Energy has a history of strategic acquisitions, including:

  • The acquisition of Bulb Energy (2021) for £1.1 billion.
  • The **purchase of German solar provider Solarcentury (2020).

By spinning out Kraken, Octopus Energy:

  • Creates a high-value asset that could be sold or merged with another player in the future.
  • Reduces complexity in its own operations, allowing it to focus on retail and corporate energy growth.

Potential buyers for Kraken could include:

  • Large utilities (e.g., EDF, Iberdrola) looking to expand their flexible energy capabilities.
  • Tech giants (e.g., Google, Microsoft) that are investing heavily in renewable energy procurement.
  • Private equity firms that specialize in energy transition investments.

How This Spin-Out Affects Different Stakeholders

The Kraken spin-out will have different implications for various groups, from investors to pension funds to energy consumers.

1. Impact on Investors: Opportunities & Risks

Opportunities:

✅ Dual-Listing Potential: If Kraken remains listed on exchanges (e.g., London Stock Exchange, Euronext), investors could gain exposure to a high-growth energy flexibility sector without needing to buy Octopus Energy itself.

✅ Higher Valuation Clarity: Kraken’s $8.65 billion valuation provides a clear benchmark for investors, making it easier to assess its fundamentals and growth potential.

✅ Access to Institutional Capital: Kraken may attract private equity, sovereign wealth funds, and pension funds looking for high-return energy investments.

✅ Dividend Potential: If Kraken becomes a standalone public company, it may increase dividends to attract income-focused investors.

Risks:

⚠ Market Volatility: The spin-out could lead to short-term price fluctuations as investors adjust to the new structure.

⚠ Regulatory Uncertainty: Energy markets are highly regulated, and changes in subsidy policies or grid access rules could impact Kraken’s operations.

⚠ Competition: Kraken will face intense competition from existing players like Vattenfall, NextEra Energy, and new entrants from the tech sector.

Actionable Tip for Investors:

  • Diversify exposure—consider holding both Octopus Energy and Kraken (if listed) to benefit from dual growth paths.
  • Monitor Kraken’s financials closely, especially cash flow from flexible energy services, which will drive its valuation.
  • Stay updated on regulatory changes that could affect demand response and battery storage incentives.

2. Impact on Pension Funds: A New Asset Class

Pension funds are increasingly looking for high-growth, low-volatility investments in renewable energy and infrastructure. The Kraken spin-out presents several advantages:

✔ Long-Term Growth Potential: With the global energy storage market projected to grow at 18.5% CAGR, Kraken is positioned for steady revenue growth.

✔ Inflation Hedge: Energy flexibility solutions benefit from rising energy prices, providing a natural hedge against inflation.

✔ ESG Alignment: Kraken’s focus on renewable energy and grid stability aligns with pension funds’ ESG mandates.

✔ Potential for Strategic Partnerships: Pension funds could invest directly in Kraken or partner with Octopus Energy for joint ventures in flexible energy projects.

Real-World Example: The California Public Employees’ Retirement System (CalPERS) has been actively investing in energy storage projects, recognizing their long-term stability and growth potential. A similar strategy could work for UK and European pension funds looking at Kraken.

3. Impact on Energy Consumers: Lower Costs & More Reliable Supply

While the spin-out is primarily a corporate and investment move, consumers may still benefit indirectly:

  • More Competitive Energy Prices: If Kraken’s demand response and battery storage solutions help optimize energy distribution, it could reduce wholesale prices over time.
  • Better Grid Stability: As Kraken scales its flexible energy services, it may contribute to more reliable electricity supply, reducing blackouts.
  • New Consumer Products: Kraken could launch retail-facing energy flexibility products, allowing businesses and households to save on energy costs.

Example: In the UK, Octopus Energy already offers a "Flexible Tariff" that adjusts prices based on real-time energy demand. If Kraken’s technology is integrated, consumers could see even more dynamic pricing models.
