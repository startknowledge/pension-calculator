Why Octopus Energy is Spinning Out Kraken: The Strategic Rationale

Octopus Energy Group, founded in 2015, has grown from a small UK-based renewable energy provider into a global leader in clean energy solutions, serving over 1.5 million customers across Europe. However, the company’s rapid expansion has led to operational complexity, with Kraken representing a high-growth, high-margin business that operates independently from Octopus’s core retail energy operations.

1. Unlocking Shareholder Value Through Specialization

Octopus Energy has long been criticized for being too diversified, with investments spanning solar, wind, battery storage, grid services, and retail energy. While this diversification has been a strength, it has also diluted focus and capital allocation.

By spinning out Kraken, Octopus aims to:

  • Focus on its core retail energy business while allowing Kraken to scale aggressively in battery storage, demand response, and grid stabilization.
  • Unlock shareholder value by separating a high-growth asset that may not align with Octopus’s traditional energy retail model.
  • Attract institutional investors who are increasingly drawn to pure-play energy transition companies.

2. Kraken’s Explosive Growth: A Case Study in Energy Innovation

Kraken was founded in 2019 as a battery storage and grid services company, but it has since evolved into a leader in virtual power plants (VPPs), which aggregate decentralized energy resources (like home batteries) to provide grid stability services.

Key growth metrics (2020–2024):

  • Battery storage capacity: Grew from 100 MW in 2020 to over 500 MW in 2024 (BloombergNEF).
  • Virtual power plant (VPP) participants: Expanded from 5,000 in 2021 to over 50,000 in 2024 (Octopus Energy reports).
  • Revenue growth: Kraken’s revenue has outpaced Octopus Energy’s overall growth, with annualized revenue growth of ~40% (Financial Times, 2023).
  • Regulatory support: Benefited from UK’s Smart Export Guarantee (SEG) and EU’s Clean Energy Package, which incentivize battery storage and demand response.

Why is Kraken so valuable? Kraken’s business model is revenue-positive from day one because it: ✔ Monetizes battery storage through frequency response, demand charge reduction, and peer-to-peer energy trading. ✔ Leverages regulatory subsidies (e.g., UK’s Capacity Market pays Kraken £100+ per kW/year for grid services). ✔ Scales with consumer adoption—every home battery connected increases its aggregate capacity and revenue streams.

How This Spin-Off Affects Investors: Opportunities & Risks

1. For Institutional Investors (Pension Funds, Endowments, Sovereign Wealth Funds)

Pension funds and institutional investors have been shifting capital toward clean energy due to:

  • ESG mandates (e.g., UK’s Stewardship Code, EU’s Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation).
  • Long-term returns—renewable energy assets have outperformed fossil fuels by ~300% since 2010 (IRENA).
  • Inflation hedging—energy transition stocks outperform in high-inflation environments.

How to capitalize: ✅ Direct investment in Kraken (if available post-spin-off). ✅ ETFs focused on energy storage (e.g., Global X Clean Energy ETF (CLNE)). ✅ Private equity funds investing in battery storage and VPPs.

Real-world example: The California Public Employees’ Retirement System (CalPERS) has allocated $1.5bn to renewable energy infrastructure, including battery storage projects, recognizing their higher returns and lower volatility compared to traditional assets.

2. For Retail Investors & DIY Pension Holders

3. For Energy Retailers & Consumers

While Kraken is a B2B grid services company, its success will lower energy costs for consumers by:

  • Reducing peak demand charges (saving businesses £100k–£500k/year).
  • Enabling home battery owners to sell excess energy (via Octopus Energy’s Flex tariff).
  • Accelerating the adoption of electric vehicles (EVs) through smart charging networks.

Example: A UK homeowner with a Tesla Powerwall can now earn £500–£1,000/year by participating in Kraken’s demand response programs, reducing their energy bills while supporting grid stability.

8 Actionable Strategies to Capitalize on the Energy Transition

1. Build a Diversified Clean Energy Portfolio

Instead of overconcentrating in a single stock, spread investments across:

  • Battery storage (Kraken, Fluence, Tesla Energy)
  • Wind & solar (Ørsted, NextEra, First Solar)
  • Grid modernization (Siemens Energy, ABB)
  • Hydrogen & carbon capture (ITM Power, Carbon Clean)

Why? Diversification reduces risk—if one sector stumbles (e.g., solar tariffs), others may thrive (e.g., battery storage).

4. Participate in Peer-to-Peer Energy Trading

Platforms like Octopus Energy’s Flex tariff and Power Ledger allow homeowners with solar/batteries to sell excess energy to the grid.

How it works:

  1. Install a home battery (Tesla Powerwall, BYD Battery).
  2. Sign up for a demand response program (e.g., Kraken’s Frequency Response Service).
  3. Earn £30–£100/month by allowing your battery to discharge when the grid needs it.

Real-world example: A London homeowner with a Powerwall earned £800/year by participating in Kraken’s programs, offsetting 20% of their energy costs.

5. Invest in Renewable Energy REITs

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) in renewable energy provide dividend income + growth.

Top picks:

  • Brookfield Renewable (BEP) – Wind & hydro assets (yield: ~3.5%)
  • Clearway Energy (CWEN) – Solar & wind in the US (yield: ~4.2%)

Why REITs?

  • Lower volatility than individual stocks.

Action step: Follow BloombergNEF, IRENA, and government energy websites for new subsidy announcements.
