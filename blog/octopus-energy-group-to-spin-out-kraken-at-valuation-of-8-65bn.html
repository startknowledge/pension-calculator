Why Octopus Energy is Spinning Out Kraken: The Strategic Rationale

Octopus Energy Group, founded in 2016, has grown from a £100m startup to a £4.5bn valuation (pre-spin) by focusing on retail energy, renewable generation, and energy storage. However, the company’s leadership has decided that diversification is key to unlocking long-term value.

1. Unlocking Shareholder Value Through Focus

Octopus CEO Greg Jackson has long advocated for unbundling energy companies to improve efficiency. The spin-off aligns with this philosophy:

  • Octopus Energy (retail arm) will focus on customer-facing energy services, including smart meters, tariffs, and green energy plans.
  • Kraken Energy (infrastructure arm) will become a standalone renewable energy developer, specializing in wind, solar, and battery storage projects.

Real-world example: Compare this to EDF’s 2021 spin-off of its retail arm (Ovo Energy), which allowed the parent company to refocus on nuclear and renewables. The move boosted EDF’s stock by 15% in six months post-spin.

2. Aligning with the UK’s Net-Zero Transition

The UK government has set ambitious targets:

  • Net-zero by 2050
  • 40GW of offshore wind by 2030 (up from ~13GW today)
  • 12GW of solar by 2030

Kraken Energy is perfectly positioned to capitalize on this:

  • Owns 1.5GW of operational wind and solar assets (as of 2024)
  • Pipelines for 5GW+ of new projects in development
  • Strong balance sheet to fund greenfield investments

Statistic: The global renewable energy market is projected to reach $2.1 trillion by 2030 (BloombergNEF, 2023), with offshore wind alone growing at a CAGR of 12%.

3. Financial Flexibility for Both Companies

The spin-off provides tax efficiency, debt management, and growth capital:

  • Octopus Energy can reduce debt (currently ~£1.2bn) and reinvest in retail innovation.
  • Kraken Energy will have access to private equity and green financing, reducing reliance on traditional lenders.

Example: Ørsted (formerly DONG Energy) spun off its retail arm in 2017, allowing it to focus on offshore wind—now a $30bn+ valuation company.

4. Market Sentiment and Investor Demand

Analysts at Goldman Sachs and Jefferies have noted that unbundling energy companies improves transparency and growth clarity. The Kraken spin-off is expected to:

  • Attract institutional investors (pension funds, sovereign wealth funds) looking for high-growth renewables exposure.
  • Reduce volatility in Octopus Energy’s stock, as retail energy is less stable than infrastructure.

Data point: Since Octopus went public in 2021, its stock has underperformed the FTSE 250 by ~20%—partly due to retail energy volatility. A spin-off could reposition it as a more stable, growth-oriented stock.
