Understanding the Spin-Out: What Is Kraken, and Why Is Octopus Separating It?

Who Is Kraken, and What Does It Do?

Kraken, a subsidiary of Octopus Energy Group, is a wholesale energy trading and grid services company focused on:

  • Battery storage solutions (including virtual power plants and grid stabilization)
  • Wholesale energy trading (buying and selling electricity in bulk markets)
  • Demand response programs (helping businesses reduce energy costs by adjusting usage during peak times)
  • Carbon offsetting and renewable energy aggregation

Unlike Octopus Energy’s retail arm, which sells energy directly to households and small businesses, Kraken operates in the B2B (business-to-business) energy market, dealing with utility companies, industrial clients, and grid operators.

Why Is Octopus Spinning Out Kraken?

Octopus Energy Group has grown rapidly since its founding in 2015, expanding from a UK-based renewable energy supplier into a global leader in clean energy innovation. However, the company has faced regulatory scrutiny, scaling challenges, and the need for greater financial flexibility.

The spin-out of Kraken is part of a broader strategy to:

  1. Unlock shareholder value – By separating Kraken, Octopus can raise capital independently, allowing both entities to focus on their core strengths.
  2. Enhance financial transparency – Kraken’s high-growth, high-risk trading operations may have been diluting Octopus Energy’s retail-focused balance sheet.
  3. Accelerate innovation in grid flexibility – Kraken’s expertise in battery storage and demand response is becoming increasingly valuable as governments push for decarbonized grids.
  4. Align with ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) trends – Investors are increasingly favoring companies with clear sustainability focus areas, and Kraken’s role in grid decarbonization fits this narrative.
