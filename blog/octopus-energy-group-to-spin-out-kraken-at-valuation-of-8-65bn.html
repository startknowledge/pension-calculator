Why Octopus Energy is Spinning Out Kraken: The Strategic Rationale

Octopus Energy Group, founded in 2015, has grown into one of the UK’s fastest-growing energy suppliers, serving over 2 million customers and generating £1.5bn in revenue in 2023 alone (Octopus Energy Annual Report, 2023). However, the company has long been divided between two core businesses:

  1. Retail energy supply (selling electricity and gas to consumers)
  2. Renewable energy generation and grid services (including wind, solar, and battery storage)

The spin-out of Kraken—Octopus’s battery storage and grid services arm—is a deliberate separation to allow each business to focus on its core strengths while unlocking greater value for shareholders.

Key Reasons Behind the Spin-Out

1. Unlocking Higher Valuations for Specialized Assets

Kraken operates in a high-growth, high-margin sector: battery energy storage systems (BESS) and grid services. These assets are critical for integrating renewable energy into the grid, as they provide flexibility, stability, and demand response—services that are becoming increasingly valuable as the UK and Europe phase out fossil fuels.

By spinning out Kraken, Octopus Energy can:

  • Attract investors who specialize in energy infrastructure (rather than retail energy)
  • Avoid diluting Kraken’s valuation by keeping it under the broader Octopus umbrella
  • Access capital markets more efficiently for future expansions

2. Faster Growth in a High-Stakes Sector

The global battery storage market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 21.5% from 2024 to 2030, reaching $30.5bn by 2030 (MarketsandMarkets, 2024). Kraken is well-positioned to capitalize on this growth, but as a subsidiary of Octopus, it may have faced operational constraints—such as regulatory hurdles or limited access to specialized funding.

A standalone Kraken can:

  • Pursue aggressive M&A (mergers and acquisitions) to dominate the UK and European storage market
  • Secure long-term contracts with grid operators for frequency response and demand management
  • Innovate faster in AI-driven grid optimization and virtual power plants (VPPs)

3. Reducing Retail Energy Risks

Octopus Energy’s retail arm operates in a highly regulated, price-volatile market. Wholesale energy prices have fluctuated wildly in recent years—spiking to £400/MWh in 2022 before dropping to £50/MWh in 2024 (Ofgem, 2024). By separating Kraken, Octopus can:

  • Isolate retail risks from its high-growth infrastructure assets
  • Focus on customer acquisition and loyalty programs without being bogged down by grid and storage investments
  • Potentially offer Kraken as a separate investment opportunity for institutional investors

4. Aligning with Government and EU Energy Policies

The UK’s Net Zero Strategy and the EU’s Green Deal Industrial Plan both emphasize grid flexibility and storage as critical enablers for renewable energy adoption. Kraken’s expertise in:

  • Battery storage for renewable integration
  • Demand response programs
  • Grid stabilization services

makes it a strategic asset for governments looking to decarbonize energy systems. A standalone Kraken could:

  • Secure public-private partnerships (PPPs) for large-scale storage projects
  • Leverage government grants and subsidies more effectively
  • Become a preferred partner for national grid operators

The $8.65bn Valuation: What Does It Mean?

The $8.65 billion valuation of Kraken is not just a number—it’s a statement about the future of energy storage. To put this into perspective:

  • It’s larger than the market cap of some major UK energy companies, including British Gas (part of Centrica) and SSE Renewables.
  • It reflects the growing recognition of storage as the "missing link" in renewable energy adoption.
  • It positions Kraken as a potential "unicorn" in the energy infrastructure space, comparable to companies like Tesla Energy or NextEra Energy.

How Was the Valuation Determined?

Kraken’s valuation is based on several key financial and strategic factors:

1. Revenue Growth and Profitability

Kraken’s revenue streams include:

  • Battery storage leasing and operation (e.g., contracts with National Grid ESO)
  • Demand response services (earning revenue by adjusting energy usage during peak demand)
  • Virtual power plant (VPP) aggregations (bundling small-scale storage to act as a large-scale generator)

In 2023, Kraken generated over £200 million in revenue (Octopus Energy Annual Report, 2023), with margins exceeding 30%—far higher than traditional energy retailers.

2. Contractual Lock-In and Recurring Revenue

Unlike retail energy, which is highly competitive and price-sensitive, Kraken’s long-term contracts with:

  • National Grid ESO (for grid services)
  • Large industrial customers (for backup power)
  • Renewable energy developers (for storage pairing)

provide stable, recurring revenue, making it a more attractive investment than volatile retail energy.

3. Scalability and Expansion Potential

Kraken is not just a storage operator—it’s a grid services innovator. Its AI-driven demand response platform and VPP aggregations allow it to:

  • Scale rapidly by adding more batteries and smart meters
  • Enter new markets (e.g., Germany, France, and the U.S.)
  • Develop hybrid projects (e.g., solar + storage + VPPs)

4. Comparable Deals in the Energy Sector

The valuation aligns with recent high-profile energy infrastructure deals:

  • NextEra Energy’s $10bn acquisition of NextEra Energy Partners (2023)
  • Tesla’s $1.5bn battery storage portfolio acquisitions (2022-2024)
  • Orsted’s $1.5bn battery storage investments (2023)

This suggests that **investors are increasingly valuing storage as a core infrastructure asset—not just a supplementary service.

How This Spin-Out Impacts Different Stakeholders

The Kraken spin-out will have different implications for investors, pensioners, energy consumers, and the broader market. Let’s break it down.

For Investors: A New Opportunity in Energy Infrastructure

If you’re an investor (whether through pension funds, ETFs, or direct shares), the Kraken spin-out creates two distinct investment opportunities:

1. Octopus Energy (Retail Focus)

  • Pros:
    • Lower volatility (retail energy is less affected by wholesale price swings)
    • Strong customer loyalty (Octopus is known for green energy and fair pricing)
    • Potential for M&A in retail energy (e.g., acquiring smaller UK suppliers)
  • Cons:
    • Regulatory risks (Ofgem price caps, net zero policies)
    • Lower growth potential compared to Kraken

2. Kraken (Storage & Grid Services)

  • Pros:
    • High-growth sector (battery storage is a $30bn+ market by 2030)
    • Recurring revenue from contracts (less dependent on volatile wholesale prices)
    • Potential for IPO or private equity investment (could attract infrastructure funds)
  • Cons:
    • Higher operational complexity (grid services require regulatory expertise)
    • Capital-intensive (needs billions in new battery deployments)

Actionable Strategy for Investors: ✅ Diversify your energy portfolio—consider both Octopus Energy and Kraken (if available post-spin-out). ✅ Monitor Kraken’s IPO plans—if it goes public, it could be a high-growth stock for the next decade. ✅ Look for ETFs or funds that invest in energy infrastructure and storage (e.g., Global X Clean Energy ETF). ✅ Watch for M&A activity—Kraken may acquire smaller storage players to dominate the market.

For Pensioners: How This Affects Your Retirement Portfolio

If you’re a pensioner (or managing a pension fund), the Kraken spin-out could indirectly impact your investments through:

1. Pension Fund Allocations to Energy Infrastructure

Many pension schemes (especially corporate and public sector pensions) hold energy-related assets. The Kraken spin-out could lead to:

  • More exposure to storage and grid services (if pension funds invest in Kraken post-spin-out).
  • Potential for higher returns if Kraken grows as expected.

Actionable Strategy for Pensioners: ✅ Check your pension fund’s asset allocation—if it holds Octopus Energy or similar energy stocks, the spin-out could boost long-term returns. ✅ Consider adding ESG-focused funds that invest in renewable energy and storage. ✅ Diversify into infrastructure ETFs (e.g., iShares Global Clean Energy ETF) to capture growth in the sector. ✅ Engage with your pension provider—some funds may offer Kraken-specific investments post-spin-out.

2. Impact on Energy Costs for Retirees

While Kraken itself doesn’t directly affect household energy bills, its grid services and storage innovations could:

  • Reduce peak demand charges (lowering bills for consumers)
  • Enable more affordable renewable energy (by smoothing out supply fluctuations)
  • Support government policies that subsidize storage for low-income households

Actionable Strategy for Pensioners: ✅ Switch to a green energy tariff (Octopus Energy offers 100% renewable plans at competitive rates). ✅ Install a small battery system (if eligible for government grants like the Smart Export Guarantee). ✅ Advocate for pension fund investments in storage—some funds may offer green bonds or infrastructure loans.

For Energy Consumers: Will Bills Go Up or Down?

One of the biggest questions for consumers is: Will this spin-out lead to higher energy bills?

The answer is complex—it depends on how Kraken operates in the future.

Potential Benefits for Consumers:

✔ More stable energy prices (if Kraken helps balance supply and demand) ✔ Cheaper renewable energy (if storage reduces reliance on fossil fuel backups) ✔ Demand response programs (where consumers earn money for reducing usage during peak times)

Potential Risks for Consumers:

❌ Higher grid charges (if Kraken’s services increase network tariffs) ❌ Regulatory delays (if Ofgem takes time to approve new storage contracts) ❌ Market consolidation (if Kraken acquires competitors, reducing competition)

Actionable Strategy for Energy Consumers: ✅ Compare energy tariffs—Octopus Energy’s fixed-rate plans may offer protection against future price hikes. ✅ Sign up for demand response programs (e.g., Octopus Energy’s "Flex" tariff, where you earn money for reducing usage at peak times). ✅ Invest in home batteries (if you have solar panels, a battery can store excess energy and reduce bills). ✅ Monitor Ofgem’s policies—if Kraken’s grid services lower wholesale prices, your bills could drop over time.
