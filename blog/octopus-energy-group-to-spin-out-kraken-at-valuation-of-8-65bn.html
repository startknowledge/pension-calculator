Octopus Energy Group to Spin Out Kraken at $8.65bn Valuation: What This Means for Investors, Pension Holders, and the UK Energy Transition

Introduction: A Landmark Moment in the UK’s Green Energy Revolution

The energy sector is undergoing one of its most significant transformations in decades, and a recent announcement by Octopus Energy Group has sent ripples through the financial markets. On June 2024, the company revealed plans to spin out its renewable energy subsidiary, Kraken Energy, in a deal valued at $8.65 billion (£6.8 billion). This move marks a bold strategic shift, positioning Kraken as a standalone entity focused on large-scale renewable energy projects, while Octopus Energy refines its core business—retail energy and flexible energy solutions.

For pension holders, investors, and energy consumers, this development carries profound implications. It signals a massive influx of capital into renewable energy, accelerates the UK’s net-zero ambitions, and could redefine how energy companies operate in the post-fossil fuel era. But what does this mean for your pension, your investments, or even your household energy bills? Let’s break it down in detail.

Why This Deal Matters: Key Statistics (2023–2024)

Before diving into the implications, let’s set the stage with some recent industry trends:

  • The UK’s renewable energy capacity grew by 12% in 2023, with solar and wind leading the charge (Department for Energy Security and Net Zero, 2024).
  • Pension funds now hold £1.2 trillion in assets tied to sustainable investments (Pensions and Lifetime Savings Association, 2024).
  • Octopus Energy was the UK’s fastest-growing energy retailer in 2023, serving 1.5 million customers (Energy UK, 2024).
  • Kraken Energy operates over 1.5 GW of renewable assets, including wind, solar, and battery storage (Kraken Energy, 2024).
  • The global renewable energy market is projected to reach $2.1 trillion by 2030, growing at a CAGR of 10% (BloombergNEF, 2024).

This spin-out is not just a corporate restructuring—it’s a macro-economic event that could influence pension fund allocations, energy market dynamics, and even government policy.

What Is Kraken Energy, and Why Is Octopus Spinning It Out?

Before analyzing the financial and strategic implications, it’s essential to understand what Kraken Energy is and how it fits into Octopus’s broader vision.

1. Kraken Energy: The Renewable Powerhouse Behind Octopus

Kraken Energy was founded in 2017 as Octopus’s renewable energy arm, specializing in:

  • Large-scale wind and solar farms (e.g., the 140 MW East Anglia One offshore wind farm, where Kraken holds a stake).
  • Battery storage projects (e.g., the 100 MW Blyth Battery Storage in Northumberland).
  • Green hydrogen initiatives (e.g., partnerships with HyDeal Ambition, a £200m hydrogen project).
  • Virtual Power Plants (VPPs)—aggregating distributed energy to balance the grid.

Unlike Octopus Energy’s retail division, which focuses on consumer energy plans and smart meters, Kraken operates in the wholesale energy market, supplying power to businesses, utilities, and even other energy companies.

2. Why Spin Out Kraken? Octopus’s Strategic Shift

Octopus Energy Group CEO Greg Jackson has stated that the spin-out is part of a "clear separation of concerns"—allowing Kraken to scale faster while Octopus focuses on customer-facing energy solutions.

Key reasons for the spin-out: ✅ Unlocking Value – Kraken’s assets are undervalued in Octopus’s current structure. A standalone listing will allow investors to directly bet on renewable energy growth. ✅ Accelerated Growth – Kraken can raise capital independently, funding more projects without diluting Octopus’s retail business. ✅ Regulatory & Market Focus – The wholesale energy market has different risks (e.g., grid constraints, policy changes) than retail, making separation more efficient. ✅ Investor Demand – Institutional investors (e.g., pension funds, sovereign wealth funds) are increasingly seeking pure-play renewables, and Kraken fits this profile.

3. The $8.65bn Valuation: What Does It Really Mean?

At first glance, $8.65 billion seems like a massive number. But let’s contextualize it:

  • Kraken’s revenue in 2023: ~£500 million (pre-tax).
  • Octopus Energy’s total valuation: ~£10 billion (including retail and Kraken).
  • Comparable renewable energy companies:
    • Ørsted (Denmark): Valued at €25bn (2024), with a similar offshore wind focus.
    • NextEra Energy (US): Valued at $120bn, the world’s largest renewable energy company.

So, is $8.65bn a fair valuation?

  • Yes, if Kraken’s growth potential is considered:
    • The UK’s offshore wind target is 40 GW by 2030 (up from ~12 GW today).
    • Kraken’s battery storage projects are critical for grid stability as renewables dominate.
    • Green hydrogen is expected to be a £17bn industry by 2030 (UK Hydrogen Strategy, 2023).
  • No, if compared to pure-play renewables:
    • Some analysts argue Ørsted trades at a higher multiple due to its global scale.

Bottom Line: The valuation reflects confidence in Kraken’s ability to execute on UK and European renewable expansion, but market sentiment will determine if it’s undervalued or fairly priced.

The Kraken spin-out creates new opportunities and risks for investors, pension funds, and energy consumers. Here’s how you can position yourself strategically.

  • Index funds (e.g., FTSE 100, S&P 500) that include energy companies.

What to do: ✔ Check your pension statement for energy-related holdings. ✔ Contact your pension provider to see if they hold Octopus Energy or Kraken shares (post-spin-out). ✔ Consider switching to a fund with higher renewable energy exposure if your current fund is underweight.

5. Understand the Tax Implications of Energy Sector Investments

Investing in renewable energy can have tax advantages, but there are also potential pitfalls.

Key Considerations: ✔ Capital Gains Tax (CGT) – If you sell Kraken shares at a profit, you’ll pay CGT (10% or 20% in the UK). ✔ Dividend Tax – Kraken may pay dividends, which are taxed at 8.75% (basic rate), 33.75% (higher rate), or 39.35% (additional rate). ✔ Pension Tax Relief – If your pension invests in renewable energy, tax relief may apply, but check if it’s EIS (Enterprise Investment Scheme) or SEIS (Seed Enterprise Investment Scheme) eligible.

How to Optimize:

  • Use your ISA or SIPP to invest in renewable energy funds—no tax on gains or dividends.
  • Consult a financial advisor before making large energy sector bets.

Kraken’s success depends on UK and EU energy policies. Key areas to monitor:

  • Offshore Wind Subsidies – The Contract for Difference (CfD) auctions determine how much Kraken can charge for renewable energy.
  • Green Hydrogen Grants – The £7bn Net Zero Hydrogen Fund could boost Kraken’s hydrogen projects.
  • Grid Connection Delays – If the National Grid can’t keep up, Kraken’s projects may face construction slowdowns.

Actionable Strategy:

  • **Follow the UK’s Energy White Paper (2024) and Net Zero Strategy updates.
  • Set up alerts for CfD auction results (published by Ofgem).

7. Consider Ethical & ESG Investing Alignments

If you’re an ethical investor, Kraken’s spin-out is a positive signal—but not all renewable energy companies are equal.

How to Ensure Your Investments Are Truly Green: ✔ Check Kraken’s ESG ratings (e.g., MSCI ESG, Sustainalytics). ✔ Avoid "greenwashing"—some companies claim to be "renewable" but still rely on gas peaker plants. ✔ Look for certifications like RE100 (companies committed to 100% renewables).

Real-World Example: Unilever has committed to 100% renewable energy by 2030, partly through partnerships with Kraken-like companies. If your pension invests in Unilever, you may already have indirect exposure.

8. Prepare for Potential Market Volatility

Energy stocks are inherently volatile, and Kraken’s IPO could see sharp price swings.

How to Mitigate Risk: ✔ Dollar-cost averaging (DCA) – Invest small amounts regularly rather than all at once. ✔ Diversify – Don’t put all your energy sector money into Kraken; hold Ørsted, NextEra, and renewable ETFs. ✔ Set stop-loss orders – If Kraken’s stock drops 20% from purchase price, sell automatically. ✔ Stay informed – Follow Bloomberg, Reuters, and Kraken’s investor updates.

Common Mistake: Assuming past performance predicts future results. Ørsted’s stock dropped 30% in 2022 due to interest rate hikes and inflation—even a "green" leader isn’t immune.
