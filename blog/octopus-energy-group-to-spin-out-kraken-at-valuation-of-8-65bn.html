Understanding the Kraken Spin-Out: What’s Happening and Why?

What Is Kraken, and Why Is Octopus Spinning It Out?

Octopus Energy Group, a £3.5 billion+ valuation company (as of 2024), has built itself as a leader in renewable energy retail and trading in the UK. Its Kraken subsidiary was originally established to trade electricity and gas wholesale, providing the backbone for Octopus’s retail operations.

The spin-out means Kraken will become an independent company, listed on the London Stock Exchange (LSE), with Octopus Energy retaining its retail and renewable energy generation assets.

Key Reasons Behind the Spin-Out

  1. Unlocking Shareholder Value

    • Kraken’s wholesale trading business is highly profitable but less growth-oriented than Octopus’s retail and renewable expansion.
    • By separating Kraken, Octopus can focus on scaling its green energy ambitions while allowing investors to trade Kraken’s stable cash flows independently.

  2. Attracting ESG and Institutional Investors

    • Kraken’s wholesale energy trading is a lower-risk, high-margin business compared to Octopus’s renewable energy projects, which carry longer payback periods.
    • Institutional investors (including pension funds) may prefer Kraken’s more predictable returns, while Octopus can attract ESG-focused capital for its green energy push.

  3. Regulatory and Market Efficiency

    • The UK energy market is becoming more fragmented, with independent system operators (ISOs) and new renewable energy players emerging.
    • A standalone Kraken can navigate wholesale markets more efficiently, reducing Octopus’s operational complexity.

  4. Preparing for a Post-Fossil Fuel Future

    • Kraken’s trading platform will play a crucial role in balancing supply and demand as the UK transitions to 100% renewable electricity by 2035.
    • By spinning out, Octopus can reinvest in offshore wind, battery storage, and green hydrogen, while Kraken handles the real-time energy trading needed for grid stability.

How This Spin-Out Affects Energy Prices and Consumer Bills

One of the most pressing concerns for UK households and pensioners is: Will this spin-out lead to higher energy bills?

Short-Term vs. Long-Term Impact on Energy Costs

Short-Term: Potential for Price Volatility

  • Wholesale energy markets are already highly sensitive to geopolitical risks (e.g., Russia-Ukraine war, Middle East tensions) and supply chain disruptions.
  • Kraken’s independence could lead to short-term price fluctuations as the market adjusts to a new trading entity.
  • Retail energy prices (what consumers pay) are heavily influenced by wholesale costs, so any instability in Kraken’s trading could temporarily push bills up.

Real-World Example: In 2022, when UK wholesale gas prices spiked due to the Ukraine war, energy retailers passed on costs to consumers, leading to record-high bills. If Kraken’s trading operations face liquidity or supply chain issues, a similar scenario could repeat—but this time with a new market player.

Long-Term: A Path to Lower, More Stable Costs

  • Kraken’s wholesale trading expertise will help optimize renewable energy procurement, reducing reliance on fossil fuel imports.
  • As offshore wind and battery storage scale, renewable energy costs continue to fall (already ~50% cheaper than fossil fuels in the UK, per BloombergNEF, 2024).
  • A more efficient energy market (with Kraken’s trading capabilities) could reduce price spikes over time.

Actionable Tip: If you’re a pensioner or long-term energy consumer, consider: ✔ Locking in fixed-rate energy deals (where available) to hedge against short-term volatility. ✔ Switching to a renewable energy tariff (many providers now offer 100% green electricity at competitive rates). ✔ Monitoring Kraken’s trading performance post-IPO—if it stabilizes wholesale costs, long-term bills may drop.

Investment Opportunities: How Pension Funds and Individuals Can Benefit

The Kraken spin-out creates multiple investment avenues for those looking to diversify into clean energy.

1. Direct Investment in Kraken (Post-IPO)

  • Kraken will be listed on the LSE, allowing retail and institutional investors to buy shares.
  • Why invest?
    • Stable cash flows from wholesale energy trading.
    • Exposure to UK energy transition without the volatility of Octopus’s renewable projects.
    • Potential for dividends (if the company maintains strong profitability).

How to Invest:

  • Open a stockbroking account (e.g., Hargreaves Lansdown, AJ Bell, Trading 212).
  • Set up a regular investment plan (RIP) to dollar-cost average into Kraken shares.
  • Consider ESG-focused funds that may include Kraken post-spin-out.

2. Pension Fund Allocations to Energy Transition

  • UK pension funds (managing £1.2 trillion+) are under increasing pressure to divest from fossil fuels and invest in renewable energy.
  • Opportunities:
    • Octopus Energy’s retained assets (retail + renewables) remain a high-growth bet on the UK’s net-zero future.
    • Kraken’s trading platform could be a core holding for funds focused on energy market efficiency.
    • Green bond funds (e.g., Octopus Renewable Power Fund) may see increased demand.

Actionable Strategy:

  • If you’re a pension scheme trustee, consider:
    • Allocating 5-10% of assets to Kraken post-IPO (if aligned with your fund’s ESG strategy).

    • Engaging with the company to ensure Kraken’s trading supports grid stability and renewable integration.

3. Private Equity and Venture Capital in Renewable Energy

  • Kraken’s wholesale trading expertise could attract private equity firms looking to scale renewable energy projects.
  • Opportunities for angel investors:
    • Early-stage renewable energy startups (e.g., battery storage, green hydrogen) may receive funding boosts from Kraken’s trading infrastructure.
    • Farmers and landowners could benefit from new renewable energy leasing models facilitated by Kraken’s market access.

Real-World Example: In 2023, Octopus Energy raised £1.5 billion from private investors to fund offshore wind and solar farms. A similar Kraken-backed financing round could emerge, creating high-growth investment opportunities.

4. Corporate Energy Procurement for Businesses

  • Large corporations (e.g., Unilever, Tesco, Amazon) are locking in long-term renewable energy deals to meet net-zero targets.
  • Kraken’s trading platform could help businesses:

    • Access cheaper renewable energy through wholesale market efficiency.

Actionable Tip for Businesses:

  • If you’re a corporate decision-maker, explore:

8 Actionable Strategies to Navigate the Kraken Spin-Out

1. Diversify Your Energy Investments

Problem: Relying solely on one energy company (even Octopus) exposes you to sector risk. Solution:

  • Spread investments across:
    • Kraken (wholesale trading)
    • Octopus Energy (retail + renewables)
    • Other renewable energy stocks (e.g., Ørsted, Vattenfall, NextEra Energy)
    • Green bond ETFs (e.g., iShares Global Clean Energy ETF)

Why It Works:

  • If Kraken faces short-term market turbulence, your Octopus or renewable ETF holdings can offset losses.

2. Use a Pension Calculator to Assess Energy Exposure

Problem: Many pensioners don’t realize how much of their portfolio is tied to energy. Solution:

  • Use a pension calculator (like those on MoneyHelper, PensionBee, or Hargreaves Lansdown) to:
    • Check your current energy sector allocation.
    • Simulate how a Kraken investment would impact your retirement income.
    • Adjust allocations to balance risk (e.g., 30% energy, 40% equities, 30% bonds).

Example: If your pension is 60% in UK equities, shifting 10% into Kraken could diversify risk while still benefiting from the UK energy transition.

3. Lock in Fixed-Rate Energy Deals Before Price Spikes

Problem: Wholesale energy volatility can lead to retail price hikes. Solution:

  • Switch to a fixed-rate tariff (if available) to hedge against short-term Kraken-related price swings.
  • Compare providers (e.g., Octopus Energy, Bulb, Ovo) for the best fixed-term deals.

Action Step:

  • Use energy price comparison tools (e.g., Uswitch, Compare the Market) to find the cheapest fixed-rate plan.
  • Set a reminder to switch 6-12 months before your contract ends to avoid last-minute price hikes.

4. Invest in Renewable Energy Infrastructure

Problem: Kraken’s trading is stable, but renewable energy projects carry growth potential. Solution:

  • Allocate 5-10% of your investable assets to:
    • Offshore wind farms (e.g., Octopus’s Hornsea Project 2).

    • Green hydrogen (e.g., ITM Power, ITM Hydrogen).

Why It Works:

  • Renewable energy assets have higher long-term growth than wholesale trading.
  • Kraken’s trading platform will benefit from increased renewable energy supply, potentially lowering costs for consumers.

5. Monitor Kraken’s Trading Performance Post-IPO

Problem: Investors may not track Kraken’s wholesale market impact. Solution:

  • Follow Kraken’s financial reports (post-IPO) for:
    • Profit margins (should remain strong if trading efficiently).
    • Renewable energy procurement volumes (should increase over time).

Where to Find Updates:

6. Consider Energy Transition Bonds for Pension Funds

Problem: Pension funds need low-risk, high-impact investments. Solution:

    • UK Green Bond Market (backed by renewable projects).

Why It Works:

Problem: Many pensioners don’t know how their funds are invested. Solution:
