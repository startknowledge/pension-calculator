Why Octopus Energy Is Spinning Out Kraken at $8.65 Billion

1. Understanding the Octopus Energy Group and Kraken’s Role

Octopus Energy Group is a UK-based energy company that has grown rapidly by focusing on renewable energy, smart grids, and customer-centric pricing. Founded in 2015, it has expanded into electric vehicle (EV) charging, battery storage, and wholesale energy trading.

Kraken, however, is a wholesale energy trading arm that deals with large-scale energy procurement, power purchase agreements (PPAs), and grid services. Unlike Octopus Energy’s retail operations, Kraken operates in B2B markets, supplying energy to businesses, industrial clients, and even other energy firms.

The spin-off means:

  • Kraken will become an independent company, listed separately on the stock market.
  • Octopus Energy will retain its retail and consumer-facing businesses, including its popular fixed-price energy plans and EV charging network.
  • Investors will gain clarity on the financial health of both entities, potentially leading to higher valuations for Kraken due to its wholesale energy expertise.

2. The Strategic Reasons Behind the Spin-Off

Octopus Energy’s CEO, Greg Jackson, has stated that the spin-off is part of a long-term strategy to unlock value for shareholders. Here’s why:

A. Unlocking Shareholder Value

  • Diversification: By separating Kraken, Octopus can focus on its retail growth while allowing Kraken to pursue its own growth opportunities in wholesale energy.
  • Higher Valuation Potential: Kraken’s wholesale energy trading business may be undervalued within Octopus’ broader structure. As an independent entity, it could attract private equity or strategic investors willing to pay a premium.
  • Clearer Financial Reporting: Investors will now see separate earnings statements, making it easier to assess performance.

B. Capital Market Trends Favor Spin-Offs

  • Post-pandemic investor demand for specialized energy firms has increased.
  • ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) investors are increasingly favoring renewable-focused energy companies, and Kraken’s role in wholesale renewables aligns with this trend.
  • The UK’s Net Zero strategy requires more efficient energy trading, and Kraken’s expertise in grid balancing and battery storage positions it well.

C. Competition and Regulatory Pressures

  • The UK energy market is consolidating, with major players like British Gas (Centrica), EDF, and SSE facing regulatory scrutiny.
  • By spinning out Kraken, Octopus can avoid antitrust concerns while still benefiting from synergies in renewable energy.

How This $8.65 Billion Spin-Off Affects Investors

1. What Investors Should Watch For

The spin-off creates two distinct investment opportunities:

  • Octopus Energy (Retail & Consumer Focused)
  • Kraken (Wholesale Energy Trading)

A. Octopus Energy: Growth in Retail and EV Charging

  • Retail Energy Market: Octopus dominates the UK’s fixed-price energy plans, with over 1.5 million customers (as of 2024).
  • EV Charging Expansion: The company is rapidly installing EV chargers, with plans to double its network by 2025.
  • Regulatory Risks: However, Ofgem’s price cap and political uncertainty could impact margins.

B. Kraken: A High-Growth Wholesale Energy Play

  • Wholesale Energy Trading: Kraken deals with large-scale energy contracts, including wind, solar, and battery storage.
  • Grid Services & Flexibility: The company provides demand response services, helping balance the grid during peak times.
  • Potential for M&A: Kraken’s technological edge in energy trading could make it an acquisition target for larger energy firms.

Pension funds, especially defined benefit (DB) schemes, often hold energy sector stocks for diversification and long-term growth. The Kraken spin-off could influence:

A. Portfolio Diversification

  • Octopus Energy’s retail model is less volatile than wholesale energy trading.
  • Kraken’s growth potential could outperform traditional energy stocks in the long run.

B. ESG Investing Trends

  • Sustainable pension funds may reallocate assets toward Kraken due to its renewable energy focus.
  • The Pensions Regulator (TPR) encourages schemes to reduce carbon exposure, and Kraken’s grid flexibility services align with Net Zero goals.

C. Potential for Higher Returns

Real-World Examples of Corporate Spin-Offs in Energy

1. BP’s Spin-Off of BP Renewables (2021)

  • What Happened: BP separated its renewable energy arm into a standalone company, later acquired by Engie.
  • Outcome:
    • BP’s core oil & gas business saw higher valuations due to clearer focus.
    • Renewable investors gained access to a pure-play green energy firm.
  • Lesson: Spin-offs can unlock value for both parent and subsidiary.

2. Shell’s Energy Transition Strategy (2022)

  • What Happened: Shell divested $10bn in oil & gas assets to increase renewable investments.
  • Outcome:
    • Shell’s stock price rose due to stronger ESG positioning.
    • Investors favored its renewable growth story.
  • Lesson: Energy firms that pivot to renewables see higher investor confidence.

3. Ørsted’s Transformation from Oil to Wind (2010s)

  • What Happened: Ørsted shut down its oil business and focused solely on offshore wind.
  • Outcome:
    • Stock price surged by 500%+ over a decade.
    • Pension funds and ESG investors flocked to its shares.
  • Lesson: Specialization in renewables drives long-term returns.
