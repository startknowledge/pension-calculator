Why Octopus Energy is Spinning Out Kraken: The Strategic Vision

Octopus Energy Group, founded in 2015, has grown into one of the UK’s most innovative energy providers, specializing in renewable electricity, battery storage, and smart energy solutions. However, the company has always seen itself as more than just an energy retailer—it’s a platform for the energy transition.

The Kraken Subsidiary: A Game-Changer in Energy Trading

Kraken, acquired by Octopus in 2021, is a wholesale energy trading and market-making firm that operates across Europe. Unlike traditional energy companies that focus solely on retail sales, Kraken specializes in:

  • Battery storage and virtual power plants (VPPs)
  • Renewable energy aggregation and trading
  • Grid flexibility services (helping balance supply and demand in real time)
  • Carbon-neutral energy solutions for industrial and commercial clients

The spin-off is not just a financial move—it’s a strategic separation to allow Kraken to scale faster, attract more investment, and compete globally without the constraints of Octopus Energy’s broader retail operations.

The $8.65 Billion Valuation: What Does It Mean?

At $8.65 billion, Kraken is being valued as a standalone energy trading giant, comparable to established players like Statkraft (Norway), Vattenfall (Sweden), or even some of the world’s largest renewable energy developers.

But why this valuation?

  1. Market Demand for Renewable Energy Trading – With governments worldwide committing to net-zero by 2050, the need for flexible, renewable energy trading platforms has never been higher.
  2. Battery Storage Boom – The global battery storage market is projected to reach $150 billion by 2030 (BloombergNEF, 2023), and Kraken is positioned to dominate this space.
  3. Grid Decarbonization – Traditional grids are struggling with intermittency issues (wind and solar aren’t always available). Kraken’s virtual power plants and demand response solutions provide the flexibility needed for a 100% renewable grid.
  4. Investor Confidence in Energy Transition – Institutional investors are increasingly allocating capital to energy transition plays, and Kraken’s spin-off signals strong confidence in its growth potential.
