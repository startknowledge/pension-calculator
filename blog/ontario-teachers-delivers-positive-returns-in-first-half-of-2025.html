Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

While the Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan's success is impressive, it is not without its challenges. Here are some common mistakes that investors can make, and how to avoid them:

  • Over-diversification: While diversification is important, over-diversification can lead to a lack of focus and poor returns. Investors should aim to diversify their portfolios, but avoid over-diversification by focusing on a core set of investments.
  • Under-diversification: On the other hand, under-diversification can lead to excessive risk and poor returns. Investors should aim to diversify their portfolios to minimize risk and maximize returns.
  • Lack of long-term focus: Investors who focus too much on short-term gains can miss out on long-term opportunities. Investors should take a long-term view, rather than trying to time the market or make quick profits.
  • Insufficient education and research: Investors who do not stay informed about market developments and trends can make poor investment decisions. Investors should prioritize education and research, staying up-to-date with the latest market trends and research.
  • Failure to rebalance: Investors who fail to rebalance their portfolios can miss out on opportunities and take on excessive risk. Investors should regularly rebalance their portfolios to ensure that they remain aligned with their investment objectives.
