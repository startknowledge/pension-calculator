Understanding the Pension System in India (Pre- and Post-1st July 2025)

Before diving into the Pension Calculator W.e.f 01/07/2025, let’s first understand the current pension structure and how it’s evolving.

1. Employees’ Provident Fund (EPF) & Employees’ Pension Scheme (EPS)

The EPF-EPS is a mandatory retirement savings scheme for salaried employees in India. Key features include:

  • Contribution Split: Employer contributes 12% of basic salary + DA, while the employee contributes 10% (8% to EPF and 2% to EPS).
  • EPS Pension: After 10 years of service, employees get a monthly pension based on their salary and years of service.
  • Current EPS Pension Formula:
    Pension = (Basic Salary / 70) × Years of Service × 15
    (Example: If your basic salary is ₹25,000 and you’ve worked for 20 years, your pension would be ₹10,714/month.)

2. National Pension System (NPS) – Tier I & Tier II

The NPS is a voluntary retirement savings scheme with two tiers:

  • Tier I (Pension Account): Tax-deductible under Section 80C (₹1.5L limit) + Section 80CCD (₹50,000 additional deduction).
  • Tier II (Flexible Account): No tax benefits, but liquidity is higher.

3. Key Changes W.e.f 1st July 2025

The EPFO and NPS are expected to introduce the following modifications:

Change Impact on Employees
Increased EPS Coverage The minimum salary threshold for EPS contributions may be raised from ₹15,000 to ₹21,000 (proposed).
Higher Pension Calculation The 70th of the salary factor may be adjusted to 60 (or another lower number), increasing pension payouts.
Auto-Enrollment in NPS Employees may be auto-enrolled in NPS Tier I with a default 10% contribution, unless opted out.
Tax Benefits Expansion Section 80CCD(2) may allow higher employer contributions (currently capped at 10% of salary).
Flexible Withdrawal Rules Partial withdrawals before retirement may be allowed under specific conditions (e.g., medical emergencies).

Why does this matter?

  • If you’re close to retirement, these changes could boost your monthly pension.
  • If you’re younger, optimizing NPS contributions and EPF-EPS will be even more critical.
  • Tax planning will need adjustments to maximize deductions under the new rules.

Strategy 2: Leverage NPS Tier I for Higher Tax Savings

  • Best For: High-income earners (₹15L+) who can afford extra contributions.
